Summary

68.1% adoption across 511.5K mentions (95% CI: [96.2%, 96.6%]). Wikipedia leads at 98%, while Google Ngrams lags at 0%.

Volume spikes detected: YouTube 2023: 446 mentions (9x volume spike), 31% adoption; YouTube 2020: 45 mentions (5x volume spike), 44% adoption. These correlate with major cultural or geopolitical events that drive public discourse and spelling adoption.

Collocation analysis (PMI-ranked): "Kiev cake" distinctively co-occurs with kyiv; "Kyiv cake" clusters with hazelnut, meringue, kiev, ukrainian; both forms share recipe. These patterns reveal how each spelling form lives in distinct discourse communities.

Context matters: "Kyiv cake" dominates in general news contexts (80% UA share), while "Kiev cake" persists strongest in food cuisine (51% RU share). Domain-specific adoption varies by up to 32 percentage points.

AI models show 75% alignment (TAS) with 78% spontaneous generation — a 3pp recognition-recall gap indicating pre-training corpus inertia.

Recommendation: Adoption is strong (68%) with mainstream momentum. remaining holdouts in Google Ngrams likely reflect legacy content rather than active resistance. LLM training data updates would increase AI alignment from current 78% free recall.
