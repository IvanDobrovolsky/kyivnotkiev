Summary

78.3% adoption across 1.8M mentions (95% CI: [84.2%, 84.5%]). Open Library Books leads at 97%, while Google Ngrams lags at 31%.

Volume spikes detected: Reddit 2022: 84 mentions (42x volume spike), 64% adoption; YouTube 2022: 85 mentions (2x volume spike), 71% adoption. These correlate with major cultural or geopolitical events that drive public discourse and spelling adoption.

Collocation analysis (PMI-ranked): "Chernigov" distinctively co-occurs with chernigov, cmi, metochion, dimnik; "Chernihiv" clusters with strong, assembly, musical, novelty; both forms share chernihiv. These patterns reveal how each spelling form lives in distinct discourse communities.

Context matters: "Chernihiv" dominates in academic science contexts (67% UA share), while "Chernigov" persists strongest in religion (92% RU share). Domain-specific adoption varies by up to 60 percentage points.

AI models show 97% alignment (TAS) with 98% spontaneous generation — a 2pp recognition-recall gap indicating pre-training corpus inertia. Moscow Patriarchate maintains 95% preference for "Chernigov" — institutional resistance.

Recommendation: Adoption is strong (78%) with mainstream momentum. remaining holdouts in Reddit, Google Ngrams likely reflect legacy content rather than active resistance.
