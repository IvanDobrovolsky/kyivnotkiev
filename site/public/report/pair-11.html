Summary

71.1% adoption across 4.1M mentions (95% CI: [88.6%, 88.8%]). Wikipedia leads at 97%, while Reddit lags at 34%.

Volume spikes detected: Reddit 2022: 169 mentions (28x volume spike), 48% adoption; YouTube 2021: 88 mentions (2x volume spike), 53% adoption. These correlate with major cultural or geopolitical events that drive public discourse and spelling adoption.

Contrastive analysis (log-odds ratio): "Lugansk" distinctively co-occurs with church, patriarch, orthodox, kirill; "Luhansk" clusters with development, region, economic, occupied. These patterns reveal how each spelling form lives in distinct discourse communities.

Context matters: "Luhansk" dominates in academic science contexts (73% UA share), while "Lugansk" persists strongest in religion (82% RU share). Domain-specific adoption varies by up to 55 percentage points.

AI models show 84% alignment (TAS) with 60% spontaneous generation — a 25pp recognition-recall gap indicating pre-training corpus inertia. Moscow Patriarchate maintains 91% preference for "Lugansk" — institutional resistance.

Recommendation: Adoption is strong (71%) with mainstream momentum. remaining holdouts in Reddit likely reflect legacy content rather than active resistance. LLM training data updates would increase AI alignment from current 60% free recall.
