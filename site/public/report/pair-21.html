Summary

Volume spikes detected: Reddit 2022: 83 mentions (4x volume spike), 74% adoption; YouTube 2022: +24pp adoption shift (74 mentions). These correlate with major cultural or geopolitical events that drive public discourse and spelling adoption.

Contrastive analysis (log-odds ratio): "Chicken Kiev" distinctively co-occurs with homemade, butter, garlic, breast; "Chicken Kyiv" clusters with day, comments, ukraine, reddit. These patterns reveal how each spelling form lives in distinct discourse communities.

Context matters: "Chicken Kyiv" dominates in history contexts (61% UA share), while "Chicken Kiev" persists strongest in war conflict (51% RU share). Domain-specific adoption varies by up to 12 percentage points.

AI models show 46% alignment (TAS) with 0% spontaneous generation — a 75pp recognition-recall gap indicating pre-training corpus inertia.

Recommendation: Adoption is progressing but contested (36%). Google Ngrams (1%) represents the biggest opportunity for improvement. LLM training data updates would increase AI alignment from current 0% free recall.
